## 1. Purpose

Wrrapd requires transparency regarding third-party funding of disputes. This disclosure helps Wrrapd manage conflicts, settlement authority, and procedural fairness in any Dispute (as defined in the Mutual Arbitration Agreement) between Contractor and Wrrapd, including Disputes relating to the WrapStar App or Platform.

---

## 2. Definitions

**“Third-Party Litigation Funding”** means any agreement or arrangement, whether written or oral, under which a person or entity that is not a party to the Dispute (other than Contractor’s retained law firm working solely on a customary contingency, hourly, or flat-fee engagement without a non-law-firm funder’s control rights) provides or agrees to provide money, capital, or something of value to fund, finance, or underwrite Contractor’s pursuit, defense, or settlement of a Dispute with Wrrapd, in exchange for repayment, a share of proceeds, a success fee tied to the Dispute, security interest in claim proceeds, or other economic interest in the Dispute or its outcome.

Third-Party Litigation Funding includes, without limitation, litigation finance / claim funding agreements, assignment of claim proceeds to a funder, and similar arrangements with hedge funds, specialty finance companies, or other investors. It does **not** include: (a) ordinary personal or business loans unrelated to funding a Dispute with Wrrapd; (b) Contractor’s payment of Contractor’s own legal fees from Contractor’s own funds; or (c) legal expense insurance that does not transfer control of the claim to a non-insurer funder—provided that if any such arrangement nonetheless conveys to a third party a direct economic interest in claim proceeds against Wrrapd, Contractor shall disclose it.

## 4. Ongoing Duty to Update

4.1 Contractor agrees to notify Wrrapd in writing (email to the address designated in the Platform or legal@wrrapd.com, or successor address Wrrapd publishes) within **ten (10) business days** after entering into, amending, or terminating any Third-Party Litigation Funding arrangement related to a Dispute with Wrrapd.

4.2 This duty continues during Contractor’s engagement and survives as to any Dispute arising out of the WrapStar relationship.

---

## 5. Consequences of Non-Disclosure

5.1 False, incomplete, or untimely disclosure under this document is a **material breach** of the Services Agreement and may result in suspension or termination of Platform access, in addition to any other remedies available at law or equity.

5.2 Nothing in this document waives any defense, privilege, or procedural right of either Party. Disputes arising under or relating to this disclosure are subject to the **Mutual Arbitration Agreement**, including its seat in **Jacksonville, Florida**.
